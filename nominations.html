The Path to the Nobel

An in-depth guide to the world's most exclusive selection process.

Science & Literature

Strictly by invitation only. Forms are sent to specific scholars and institutions globally.

The Peace Prize

Open eligibility for qualified nominators. No invitation is required to submit a candidate.

How to Achieve a Nomination (By Category)

Physics, Chemistry, Medicine & Economics

To be nominated in these fields, your work must fundamentally change the understanding of the universe or human life.

  • Who can nominate? Only those invited by the Nobel Committee. This includes 3,000 specially selected professors, previous Laureates, and members of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences.
  • Achievement Level: Typically requires a discovery that has "stood the test of time" (often 20+ years of verification).
  • Limit: Maximum of 3 people can share a prize.
The Nobel Peace Prize

Unique because it can be awarded to individuals or organizations.

  • Who can nominate? Any person meeting these criteria:
    • Members of national governments or parliaments.
    • University professors of History, Social Science, Law, or Philosophy.
    • Former Peace Prize Laureates.
    • Current/former members of the Norwegian Nobel Committee.
  • The "Achieve" Factor: Focus on "fraternity between nations," "abolition of standing armies," or "promotion of peace congresses."
The Literature Prize

Awarded for a cumulative body of work rather than a single book.

  • Who can nominate? Professors of literature/linguistics, presidents of national writers' societies, and members of the Swedish Academy.
  • The "Achieve" Factor: Production of "the most outstanding work in an ideal direction."

The 12-Month Selection Cycle

September - January

Invitation Phase: Nominations are collected. Deadline is Jan 31.

February - August

The Sifting: Committees and experts review hundreds of nominees to create a "Short List."

October

Selection: Awarding institutions vote. Winners are announced globally.

December 10

Ceremony: Laureates receive medals in Stockholm (and Oslo for Peace).

Strict Statutes & Restrictions

Self-Nomination

If you nominate yourself, you are automatically disqualified. The Nobel Committee ignores all such entries.

Posthumous Rules

A person cannot be nominated after they have passed away. However, if they die after the announcement, the prize is still awarded.

The 50-Year Secret

The name of a nominee is never released. It remains locked in the archives for exactly 50 years before historians can access it.
